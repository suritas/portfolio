Hi, there!

I’m a UX Research, UX Design, and Project Management professional with 8+ years experience. I manage enterprise technical & non-technical projects and UX research and design for start-ups such as Floyd Home and Evva Health, and enterprises like NBC-Universal and CNBC. I’ve worked with platforms including mobile, smart, voice, and multi-device integrations. Find me “in the zone” developing research protocols, conducting analyses, or prototyping.
My educational background includes a Masters of Science in Information: User Experience & Human Computer Interaction from the University of Michigan’s premier UX Masters program and a GLSS Lean Six Sigma Black Belt.
Outside work you can find me reading, playing badminton, or enjoying the Pacific Northwest's vistas and restaurants.   


My approach

My work is guided by User Experience principles and Lean methodologies.
I am passionate about guiding teams and problem solving for the user. Driving both UX research and UX design phases in all my projects, I bridge the gap between research and design.
In practice, I often use (1) scientific and lean methods to develop a cohesive research protocol, (2) project management skills and tools such as Smartsheet, Miro, and Sharepoint to track progress, (3) tailored qualitative & quantitative research and analysis methods such as competitive/comparative analysis, affinity mapping, heuristic evaluation, contextual inquiry interviews, information architecture, process & pain point mapping, survey, task observation, and preference & A/B testing, and more to uncover findings (4) Figma, Adobe XD, and Adobe Illustrator to design and prototype solutions and to visualize data.
My unique background prior to User Experience is in (1) scientific research at the University of Washington, (2) technical & non-technical enterprise project management at the Washington State Department of Health, and (3) database training facilitation at Neighborhood House; this experience has allowed me to communicate complex processes with precision and care for users, clients, and stakeholders and see problems from a variety of perspectives.
